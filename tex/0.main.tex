\IEEEtitleabstractindextext{
\begin{abstract}
\justify{Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) has accelerated foundation-model research across multiple domains, yet graph data remains less explored than text and vision in this paradigm. This survey reviews graph prompt learning from the perspectives of cross-modality, cross-domain, and cross-task generalization, and develops a unified view based on prompt tokens, token structures, and insertion patterns. It further examines the relationship between graph pre-training and prompting, discusses how prompts align downstream tasks with pretext objectives, and summarizes representative prompt tuning strategies. The resulting taxonomy covers over one hundred representative studies through early 2026 across pre-training strategies, prompt design, multi-modal prompting, and graph domain adaptation. We also summarize application scenarios and supporting resources, including ProG and a curated repository of papers, benchmarks, and code. Finally, the survey discusses current challenges and future directions for graph prompting in graph representation learning and related AGI-oriented applications.
Relevant resources are available at \url{https://github.com/WxxShirley/Awesome-Graph-Prompt} and \url{https://github.com/sheldonresearch/ProG}.
}

\end{abstract}
